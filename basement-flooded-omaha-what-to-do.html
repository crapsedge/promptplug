Safety first: If water is near outlets, cords, appliances, or your breaker panel, do not step into it. Electricity + water can be deadly. If you’re unsure, stay out and get professional help.

Quick Action Checklist (60 seconds)

  1. Check electrical risk. If water is near electrical, stay out.
  2. Stop the water source if possible (shutoff valve / main).
  3. Document damage (photos + short notes).
  4. Move valuables to a dry area.
  5. Start airflow (fans/dehumidifier) if safe.
  6. Get connected to restoration help (routing request).

Step-by-step: What to do (detailed)

  1. Confirm it’s safe to enter.
    If water reached outlets, extension cords, appliances, or the breaker area, do not enter.
  2. Stop the water.
    Shut off the valve to the leaking fixture/pipe, or your home’s main water shutoff if needed.
  3. Take photos and notes.
    Capture the source, water level, affected rooms, and damaged items for insurance.
  4. Move what you can.
    Lift furniture legs onto blocks/towels, move boxes and electronics, remove rugs if possible.
  5. Start drying fast.
    Airflow matters. Use fans and a dehumidifier if safe. Open windows only if humidity is lower outside.
  6. Don’t ignore hidden moisture.
    Water can soak behind baseboards, drywall, insulation, and flooring. That’s where mold risk starts.
  7. Request restoration help.
    If the water is significant or contaminated, you’ll want professional extraction and drying.

Common causes in Omaha homes

  • Burst / leaking pipes (cold snaps, aging supply lines)
  • Sump pump failure (power loss, clogged discharge line)
  • Foundation seepage (heavy rain, poor grading, clogged gutters)
  • Water heater failure (tank leak, pressure valve issues)
  • Appliance leaks (washer hoses, utility sink plumbing)
Tip: If the water smells like sewage or came from a drain backup, treat it as contaminated. Avoid contact and get professional help.

FAQ

Should I use a shop vac to remove basement water?

A shop vac can help with small amounts of clean water. If the water level is high, spreading, or contaminated, professional extraction is safer and usually much faster.

How fast does mold start after flooding?

Mold risk can increase quickly when moisture stays trapped. The goal is to remove standing water and begin drying immediately, including hidden moisture behind walls and under flooring.
